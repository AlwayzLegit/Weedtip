# Weedtip Platform Audit — Nationwide Expansion (CA → 42 states, 9,433 dispensaries)
**Date:** 2026-07-04 · **Scope:** web app, Supabase schema/RLS, live production DB · **Method:** all CRITICAL/HIGH findings adversarially re-verified against code and live data; MEDIUM/LOW were single-pass only.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The platform is structurally sound — clean referential integrity, working checkout plumbing, a fully built compliance table — but the nationwide expansion shipped on top of unmodified CA-era assumptions, and it shows on nearly every surface. **Theme 1: the PostgREST 1,000-row cap.** At 9,433 rows, every unbounded select silently truncates: CA's state page drops 538 shops, ~36-58 sitemap-advertised city pages 404, /map shows at most 1,000-5,000 of 9,212 pins, the admin panel can only see/grant against 1,000 listings, and headline stats (homepage, admin) are wrong. **Theme 2: hardcoded California.** A flat 15% CA tax is baked into 9+ SQL functions plus web and mobile clients and is charged as real money via Stripe and POS; open-now is computed in UTC (search) and Pacific (detail page) for shops in 6 timezones; "near you" copy fronts nationwide unscoped lists. **Theme 3: compliance and commerce integrity are unwired.** `operating_regions` gates nothing — 1,880+ dispensaries in 17 medical-only states accept orders with zero medical-card check — buyers can PATCH their own orders to `paid`/`completed` via PostgREST (forging "verified shopper" reviews and corrupting GMV), and password reset dead-ends with no way to set a new password. **Theme 4: a content vacuum monetized and indexed anyway.** 5 products (all one NYC shop), 0 live deals, 0 owned brands, and 1 ad region — yet the sitemap emits ~37k URLs including ~2,800 deal pages whose JSON-LD tells Google "0 active deals," and brands can pay for locked 2-month featured terms in 41 states where the placement can never render. Nothing reported was refuted; roughly a week of focused work (Phase A below) eliminates the worst correctness, security, and SEO damage.
